Towards the farthest north
A supplement to the North Capet; the end point Havøysund and the islands beyond offer a encounter with a thriving community on the coast of Finnmark.
The road out to Hav&oslash;ysund is fascinatingly varied. Parts of the road traverse a barren rocky landscape with the Arctic Ocean on one side and rugged outcrops on the other. The landscape is stark and dramatic, but in the midst of this apparent barrenness you may get to see both sea eagles and reindeer. The drive is a special experience, different from anything else. Located at 71&deg; north, the little fishing village of Hav&oslash;ysund is literally the end of the road.
In combination with the Hurtigruten Coastal Express and Norwegian Scenic Route Varanger, this route can form part of one of Norway&rsquo;s most beautiful circular trips.

Norwegian Scenic Route Hav&oslash;ysund runs from Kokelv to Hav&oslash;ysund [889]. The Hurtigruten coastal express calls at Hav&oslash;ysund daily. This allows you to plan round trips combining several Norwegian Scenic Routes.
You can also extend the trip by taking the M&aring;s&oslash;y express boat (M&aring;s&oslash;yekspressen) to the Rolvs&oslash;y (Gunnarnes quay), Ing&oslash;ya or M&aring;s&oslash;ya islands. It is definitely worth the trip. If you are going to M&aring;s&oslash;ya, you must call the boat on tel: 0047 480 23 948, report whether you are going to M&aring;s&oslash;y or will be picked up there.
For cyclists: The route follows the coastline, but includes a few steep hills across the fell. Beware of strong winds. It is possible to combine Norwegian Scenic Route Hav&oslash;ysund with National Cycle Route 1. The route also coincides with EuroVelo 1 &laquo;Atlantic Coast Route&raquo;. The route is not signposted as a cycle route.

Experiences
It is the sea and fishing that has been the main industry for the people of Hav&oslash;ysund, and deep sea fishing is also what attracts many people to the area. Here at the edge of the Barents Sea you can experience the midnight sun in summer and the northern lights in winter. The adventurous should take the boat from Hav&oslash;ysund to the islands of Ing&oslash;y, Rolvs&oslash;ya and M&aring;s&oslash;y. Hjelms&oslash;ystauren, one of northern Europe's largest bird rocks, is also worth a visit.
A half-hour walk from Hav&oslash;ysund can take you to the island's highest point, Hav&oslash;ygavlen, with views towards the Arctic Ocean and nearby islands. A hike to Storvika is also an easy walk from Hav&oslash;ysund, taking you out to cliffs and skerries in the weather-beaten landscape on the southwestern part of Hav&oslash;ya. In the northeast of the island you will find the historic Russehula cave.
